Tenses are usually manifested by the use of specific forms of verbs, particularly in their conjugation patterns. The main tenses found in many languages include the past, present and future. Past Tense

Simple Past Tense

Describe an action already completed.
Can be used with or without adverbs of time.

Ex :

  • I went to Egypt in 1998.
  • He drank his whiskoy almost bottoms up.

Past ContinuousTense

Describe an action already completed.
Can be used with or without adverbs of time.

Ex :

  • Everyone was shouting
  • They were always smailling

Past perfect Tense

Describe a completed action of the past that happened before another event look place

Ex :

  • After he had finised work, he went straight home.

Past Perfect ContinuousTense

Describean action in the past that began before a certain point in the past and continued up untill that time.

Ex :

  • She had been working at that company for three years when it went out of business.




Present Tense

Simple Present Tense

Express habits or general truth.
Describ a future event as part of a plan or arrangement.

Ex :

  • I am six years old.
  • The plan arrives at 18:00 tomorrow.

Present ContinuousTense

Describe an action going on at the time of speaking.
Express temporary action which may not be happeningat the time of speaking.

Ex :

  • They are swimming in the pool.
  • Jhone is driving his father's car whil hi own car is in the workshop.

Present perfect Tense

Express past action which is not defined by a time of occurrence.
Express an action which started in the past and has continued up until now.

Ex :

  • Thara isn't at home, I think she has gone shopping.

Present Perfect ContinuousTense

Express an action which started at same point in the past and may not be complete.

Ex :

  • He has been living in Bangkok since he left the school.




Future Tense

Simple Future Tense

Express an action or circumstance which hasn't taken place yet.

Ex :

Express planned events.

Future perfect Tense
