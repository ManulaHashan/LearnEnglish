Simple Tense

Simple tense definition:
The simple tense is a tense in English with no aspect that expresses actions occurring in the past, present, and future.

〰 Simple Past Tense 〰

The simple past tense (also called the past tense) is used to express actions that are completed at any time (recent or distant past) or for any duration (length of event).

For regular verbs, add “-ed” to the end of the verb to create the simple past tense.

  • I: jumped
  • you (singular) : jumped
  • he/she/it: jumped
  • we: jumped
  • you (plural): jumped
  • they: jumped

Usage

✔ Describe an action already completed.

  • I went to Egypt in 2010

✔ Can be used with or without adverbs of time.

  • He drank his wiskey almost bottoms up

Simple past tense used in sentences:

  • My voice echoed in the cavern.
  • Garry walked to the park after he finished his meal.
  • We shared our meal with strangers.

〰 Simple Present Tense 〰

The simple present tense (all called the present tense) is used to express action that is happening in the present, now, relative to the speaker or writer.

The simple present is generally used for actions that are factual, normal, or regular in occurrence, sometimes called habitual actions. Habitual actions are actions that occur in the present but are not necessarily happening right now.

  • I: jump
  • you (singular) : jump
  • he/she/it: jumps
  • we: jump
  • you (plural): jump
  • they: jump

Usage

✔ Express habit or general thruth.

  • I 'm ' twenty-nine years old.

✔ Describe a future event as part of a plan or arrangment.

  • The plane arrives at 9pm today.

Simple present tense used in sentences:

  • She bites her nails. (habit)
  • Do not bite your nails. (direction/instruction)
  • Skunks smell (general truth)
  • I live in San Francisco. (unchanging situation)
  • We love chocolate cake. (unchanging situation)

〰 Simple Future Tense 〰

The simple future tense (also called the future tense) is used to express action that will certainly occur at any time later than now.
Add “will” or “shall” before the first person present conjugated verb to create the simple future tense.

  • I: will jump
  • you (singular): will jump
  • he/she/it: will jump
  • we: will jump
  • you (plural): will jump
  • they: will jump

Usage

✔ Express an action or circumstance which hasn't taken place yet.

  • I will meet him tomorrow.

Simple future tense used in sentences:

  • They will go to New York tomorrow.
  • You shall see a play this fall.
  • I will walk to school tomorrow.

Continuous Tense

The continuous tense shows an action that is, was, or will be in progress at a certain time. The continuous tense is formed with the verb ‘be’ + -ing form of the verb.

〰 Past Continuous Tense 〰

The Present continuous can be used to show an action which is happening at the time of speaking.

Usage

✔ Express uncompleted action of the past.

  • Everyone was shouting.

✔ Describe persistent habits of the past.

  • They were alwaysquarrelling.

〰 Present Continuous Tense 〰

The Past continuous can be used to show an action which was happening in the past. It is important to remember that the Past continuous is usually used to show an action which was happening when another action, which is usually shorter, happened at the same time, stopped the continuous action or started after the continuous action.

Usage

✔ Describe action going on at the time of speaking.

  • They are swimmingin the pool.

✔ Express temporary action which may not be happening at the time of speaking.

  • John 's driving his father's car while his own car is in the workshop.

〰 Future Continuous Tense 〰

The Future continuous is used to show that an action will be happening at a time in the future.

Usage

✔ Express what will be going on at same time in the future.

  • You 'll be missing the sunshine once you're back in England.

✔ Express planned events.

  • This time next week I will be sun-bathing in Boli.
