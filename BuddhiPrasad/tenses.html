Tenses

Tenses are forms of a verb that show the time, continuance or completion of an action or a state that is expressed in connection with the moment at which a statement is made about it. There are three main tenses.

  • Present Tenses
  • Past Tenses
  • Future Tenses

Present Tenses

Simple Present Present Perfect Present Continuous Present Perfect Continuous
We use the simple present tense when an action is happening right now, or when it happens regularly The present perfect tense refers to an action or state that either occurred at an indefinite time in the past or began in the past and continued to the present time The present continuous verb tense indicates that an action or condition is happening now, frequently, and may continue into the future. The present perfect continuous tense shows that something started in the past and is continuing at the present time.

Structure :
Subject + Verb + es/es


Structure :
Subject + Has/have + Verb


Structure :
ubject + is/am/are + Verb(+ing)


Structure :
Subject + Has/have + been + Verb(+ing)


Examples :
  • I take exercise daily.
  • She reads a book in the library.
Examples :
  • He has made this colorful chart.
  • I have walked on this path before.
Examples :
  • He is playing football.
  • I am studying in a high school.
Examples :
  • He has been working in this department since 2017.
  • I have been completing my assignment for the last three days.

Past Tenses

Simple Past Past Perfect Past Continuous Past Perfect Continuous
The simple past is a verb tense that is used to talk about things that happened or existed before now. The past perfect is a verb tense used to talk about actions that were completed before some point in the past.The past perfect tense is for talking about something that happened before something else. The past continuous tense, also known as the past progressive tense, refers to a continuing action or state that was happening at some point in the past. The past perfect continuous tense (also known as the past perfect progressive tense) shows that an action that started in the past continued up until another time in the past.

Structure :
Subject + Verb or irregular verb


Structure :
Subject + had + Verb


Structure :
Subject + was/were + Verb(+ing)


Structure :
Subject + had + been + Verb(+ing)


Examples :
  • He completed the assignment.
  • She read the newspaper..
Examples :
  • He had finished his homework.
  • Kamal had completed task.
Examples :
  • He was reading the book.
  • I was going to the park for a morning walk.
Examples :
  • He had been completing his assignment for the last two hours.
  • I I had been playing footballsince morning.

Future Tenses

Simple Future Future Perfect Future Continuous Future Perfect Continuous
The simple future is a verb tense that’s used to talk about things that haven’t happened yet. The future perfect is a verb tense used for actions that will be completed before some other point in the future. The future continuous tense is a verb tense that indicates that something will occur in the future and continue for an expected length of time. The future perfect continuous is a verb tense that describes actions that will continue up until a point in the future.

Structure :
Subject+ will/shall+ verb


Structure :
Subject + will have + verb


Structure :
Subject + will be/shall be + verb(+ing)


Structure :
Subject + will have been + verb(+ing)


Examples :
  • I shall go to the park for a walk.
  • She will perform his duty.
Examples :
  • He will have played football.
  • I will have completed my assignment.
Examples :
  • He will be playing football.
  • We shall be eating the meal.
Examples :
  • He will have been watching the football match for over fifty minutes.
